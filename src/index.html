The Halifax Explosion Die of Consequence

By Martin Baldwin

You are in Halifax the morning of December 6th, 1917.
What's your fate?

DEAD

You are buried under the rubble of collapsed buildings, not to be found until it’s too late.

DEAD

Standing outside, curious of the cause of the commotion at the harbour, you are killed by the blast instantaneously.

DEAD

You survive the blast, but are severely injured by rubble from your now-levelled house that had collapsed on you.

You manage to find shelter and medical treatment; however, within a few days, your condition worsens and kills you.

INJURED

You survive, but are blinded by flying shards of glass from your house’s broken windows.

You have your eyes removed and replaced with glass eyes, like many other Haligonians.

INJURED

The blast threw you a great distance away into a place you don’t recognize, along with a severe concussion and pieces of debris in your arm.

ALRIGHT

You’re one of the lucky ones, untouched while off at work.

However, your house has been swept away by the tsunami that ensued, and you are now homeless.

ALRIGHT

On your way to school, you end up safe from the explosion.

However, the rest of your family at home has been killed and you alone are left behind. Your mother, father, grandmother, two brothers, and sister did not survive.

ALRIGHT

On a train arriving from New Brunswick, you are saved by the incredible sacrifice of Vince Coleman.

Coleman was killed by the explosion, staying behind at his post to warn incoming trains of the danger.

Having received the warning, your train was stopped and you are left unharmed.

ALRIGHT

Miraculously, you survive the collapse of your house without significant injury.

Unfortunately, however, your house caught fire and was burned to the ground. You must now seek shelter.
